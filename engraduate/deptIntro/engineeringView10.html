Educational objectives

Core objectives
  • 1. The Department of Architecture aims to cultivate architectural experts with intellectual and design capabilities in architecture and urban planning.
  • 2. We cultivate architectural scholars with specialized knowledge in urban and architectural theories.
Detailed objectives

  • 1. Cultivate balanced architectural experts with knowledge of architectural theory and design skills

    • 1-1 Cultivate architectural design and theory experts with specialized knowledge in architectural theory and design techniques
    • 1-2 Cultivate architects who systematically acquire knowledge of architectural theory based on design practical experience

  • 2. Cultivate architectural experts with insights into historical cities and megacities based on Seoul research

    • 2-1 Cultivate urban architectural experts who can deeply analyze and reconstruct the cultural characteristics and urban architectural diversity of Seoul
    • 2-2 Cultivate international architects who expand the architectural domain to Asian megacities centered on Seoul

  • 3. Pursue in-depth knowledge of architectural theory and design, and integrated knowledge in urban and architectural fields

    • 3-1 Cultivate architectural experts who can lead design and theory fields in urban and architectural areas with advanced knowledge of architectural theory and design techniques
    • 3-2 Cultivate architectural scholars who can systematically develop theories on urban history, culture, and urban economics
Research areas Architectural planning and programming, architectural history and historical cities, modern architecture and urban architecture, architectural design and theory, housing theory, urban policy, and institutional research, integrated design, digital design

General admission degree programs

Master
  • Individuals who have obtained a bachelor's degree from domestic or foreign universities or those expected to obtain a degree upon completion of the current registered semester
  • Individuals recognized as having equivalent or higher academic qualifications under relevant laws
Doctorate
  • Individuals who have obtained a bachelor's degree from domestic or foreign universities or those expected to obtain a degree upon completion of the current registered semester
  • Individuals recognized as having equivalent or higher academic qualifications under relevant laws
Eligibility for master's/doctorate's
Support for North Korean defectors
  • Individuals qualified to apply for master's or doctorate's program
  • Individuals registered as North Korean defectors pursuant to the North Korean Defectors Protection and Settlement Support Act

Additional eligibility requirements and submission documents

  • Submission of official English proficiency test scores (TOEIC, TOEFL, TEPS, IELTS) recommended
  • Portfolio (free format) recommended: Bring it to the interview
