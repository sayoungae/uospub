Educational objectives

Core objectives The Department of Computer Science and Engineering aims to cultivate creative specialized research personnel who can lead future society and professionals with leadership to guide the information society by providing advanced computer theory and practical education.
Detailed objectives

  • 1. Advanced computer theory and practical learning

    • 1-1 Learning advanced computer theory and practical skills
    • 1-2 Cultivating experts with insight into domestic and international research

  • 2. Developing innovative design capabilities

    • 2-1 Fostering creativity to establish new theories
    • 2-2 Cultivating open-minded leaders with an international perspective

  • 3. Developing professional leadership

    • 3-1 Cultivating leaders who can predict and lead future technological advancements
    • 3-2 Cultivating open-minded leaders with an international perspective
Research areas Advanced computer fields: database, computer network, distributed computing, embedded system, operating system, high-performance storage, artificial intelligence, speech recognition, wireless multimedia, computer games, software engineering, digital watermarking, DRM, computer graphics, spline theory, GPU techniques, computational geometry, big data analysis and information retrieval, artificial intelligence, health informatics, human-computer interaction, etc.
