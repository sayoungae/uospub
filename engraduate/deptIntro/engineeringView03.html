Educational objectives

Core objectives
  • 1. The Department of Architectural Engineering approaches problem-solving related to the essence of architecture qualitatively and strives to contribute to the academic development of the architectural field by systematically acquiring this knowledge through in-depth research by professors and students in fields such as structure, construction management and execution, materials, environment, and facilities.
  • 2. In researching specialized and subdivided fields of architecture, we focus on pursuing theoretical depth while cultivating the ability to apply acquired knowledge to architectural practice and adapt to real-world situations, ultimately aiming to cultivate personnel who can create a healthy social environment in Korea's architectural academia and practical fields.
Detailed objectives

  • 1. Improving the quality of research and systematically acquiring knowledge to develop abilities as specialized technicians

    • 1-1 Activating research through participation in national support projects (e.g., ERC, BK21, and NRL)
    • 1-2 Cultivating capable technicians who can conduct independent research activities in their specific specialized fields

  • 2. Conducting integrated research in specialized and subdivided fields and construction projects to develop the ability to apply knowledge to architectural practice and adapt to real-world situations

    • 2-1 Developing practical adaptation skills through the operation of curricula necessary for practice
    • 2-2 Cultivating recognition as engineers who can solve problems with engineering thinking in society

  • 3. Cultivating personnel who can contribute to practical fields and create a healthy social environment

    • 3-1. Cultivating specialized construction technicians who can recognize the reality of the construction sector and seek solutions
    • 3-2. Developing researchers' capabilities to discover and execute key technologies and large-scale complex projects related to construction

  • 4. Expanding academic exchanges to become a world-class university

    • 4-1 Activating international academic exchanges and collaborative research with foreign universities
    • 4-2 Cultivating internationally recognized technicians through exchanges of visiting professors and students
Research areas Structural analysis, steel and composite structures, reinforced concrete structures, building facilities, building environment, construction project management, building construction

Minimum publication requirements for doctoral dissertation

  • At least one paper published as first author in a journal listed (or candidate) by the National Research Foundation of Korea
  • Or at least 1 paper published as first author in SCI(E) listed journals or higher

Graduate school operating principles
