UOS graduate school departments

Related regulations
  • Article 31 of the UOS Graduate School Implementation Regulations
Cooperating departments
  • Other UOS graduate school departments
Qualifications for completion
  • Currently enrolled graduate students (including those planning to re-enroll in the current semester)
Range of transferable credits
  • A. Master's program - Up to 12 credits during the entire enrollment period
  • B. Doctorate's program - Up to 18 credits during the entire enrollment period
    ※ Students belonging to a participating department in the interdepartmental cooperative program may take courses established in the cooperative program with the approval of their department and the head professor of the cooperative program. Similarly, students from participating departments can take courses established in the cooperative program with the approval of the supervising professor of that program. In addition, students from departments participating in interdisciplinary majors may take courses established in the interdisciplinary major with the approval of their department and the head professor of the interdisciplinary operating department. In such cases, they can earn credits for courses offered in other graduate departments without restriction.

UOS graduate schools

Related regulations
  • Article 32 of the UOS Graduate School Implementation Regulations
Cooperating departments
  • Graduate School ⇔ Special Graduate School, Professional Graduate School
Qualifications for completion
  • Currently enrolled graduate students (including those planning to re-enroll in the current semester)
Range of transferable credits
  • A total of no more than 6 credits may be transferred during the enrollment period, and a grade of B0 or higher must be achieved for the transferred courses to be recognized.
Scope of transfer course selection
  • Courses taught by full-time faculty
  • Courses not offered in the affiliated graduate school
  • Doctorate's program students are only allowed to take doctorate's courses from the affiliated graduate schools and cannot take master's courses from professional or special graduate schools.
